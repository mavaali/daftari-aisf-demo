> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Principles

A brush-pass is a contact tradecraft of last resort for documentary handover when neither a meet nor a drop is appropriate. Two cardinal rules: (a) zero verbal exchange; (b) zero eye contact.

## Standard protocol

1. **Site selection.** Public transit, busy stair, or pedestrian throughway. Density of strangers must be high enough to make a single observer's account uncertain.
2. **Approach.** Officer and asset close on opposite vectors; relative speed normal.
3. **Exchange.** Material transferred in the moment of passing; both hands return to neutral immediately.
4. **Egress.** No look-back; no acceleration; no change of direction.

## Materials

- Preferred: USB unit, sealed envelope, or single-page folded paper.
- Maximum size: pocket-volume.
- Both hands free at moment of pass.

## Asset preparation

Asset must run the protocol cold (no material) at least twice before first operational use. Officer rehearses with desk colleague.

## Failure modes

- **Drop.** Material falls. Standard response: keep walking; one party returns at a planned interval to recover. Never two parties.
- **Eye contact.** Treated as a possible compromise. Stand-down for 30 days.
