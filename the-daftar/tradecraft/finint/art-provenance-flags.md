> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Flags

### Strong
- Long silent period followed by sudden re-emergence at a different geography.
- Typography or stationer's marks inconsistent with the stated era of the consignment paperwork.
- "Sealed lot" sales where individual items are not catalogued.
- Sale to a counterparty followed by resale of the same item within 24 months at materially different valuation.

### Moderate
- Provenance text refined between consignment and catalogue.
- Auction house changes between adjacent sales without commercial rationale.
- Above-market commission rates on lower-value lots (rent-seeking signal).

### Weak (situational)
- Anonymous bidder phone-only at auction.
- Consignor "deceased / estate" attribution that cannot be traced.

## Use

These flags are an analyst's screen, not a courtroom standard. Two strong + one moderate, or three moderate, is the Bureau threshold for raising an open question on a lot.
