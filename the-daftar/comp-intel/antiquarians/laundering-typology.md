> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Canonical pattern

1. **Sequestration.** Asset enters "private collection" — provenance freezes, lot disappears.
2. **Freshening.** Geneva consignment paperwork is regenerated using a small print-shop infrastructure; provenance now reads "deaccessioned from a Swiss private holder."
3. **Catalogue laundering.** London auction catalogue text refines the provenance further; the Geneva freshening becomes invisible.
4. **Sale.** Auction is the legitimising endpoint; sale price establishes a clean mark-to-market valuation.

## Variants

- **Sealed-lot variant.** Item never enters a public catalogue; sold as part of a "sealed lot" with stated provenance covering the lot as a whole, not individual pieces.
- **Crash-and-recover variant.** Item sold quickly below estimate to a friendly counterparty; resold later at full value.

## Asset-class coverage

Historically: manuscripts, works on paper, sealed correspondence. Possible expansion: coinage (see [T5](../../tensions/tension-005.md)).
