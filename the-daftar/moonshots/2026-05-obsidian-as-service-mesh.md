> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Premise

We model OBSIDIAN GROUP as a vertically integrated private intelligence firm — principals, fixers, contractors, a single retainer book. Invert: model it as a **service-mesh** that brokers capability between independent contractors and clients.

Under the mesh model:
- Vasari is not OBSIDIAN's fixer — Vasari is a contractor who is on OBSIDIAN's mesh.
- "Anvil" is not OBSIDIAN's wet-work asset — Anvil is on the mesh, available to whoever pays.
- The "client list" is partially fictitious — many engagements never touch a central OBSIDIAN balance sheet.

## What changes?

- The two-stream payment pattern observed by DOLPHIN becomes natural: different clients, different contractors, same brokerage.
- The Geneva fronts are billing infrastructure, not operational infrastructure.
- Burning OBSIDIAN does not burn the mesh — the contractors re-tile under a new brand.

## Indicators

- Vasari's apparent freedom to travel and represent without obvious central tasking.
- Anvil's call-sign appearing in OBSIDIAN-adjacent traffic without OBSIDIAN-attributable billing.
- The diversity of services in the [catalogue](../comp-intel/obsidian-group/services-catalog.md) — wider than a single firm's pedigree would explain.

## Implication

If OBSIDIAN is a mesh, our targeting strategy needs to be contractor-by-contractor, not firm-by-firm. The firm is the brand; the mesh is the threat.
