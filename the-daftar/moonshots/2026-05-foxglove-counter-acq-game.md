> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Premise

Counter-acquisition watch operations are usually run as detection problems — find the intermediated buyer; raise a flag. Reframe as a multi-round game between us and the procurement actor.

## Game structure

- The adversary's move: choose an intermediation depth (shell levels).
- Our move: choose an investigation depth (how many layers we unwind).
- Their cost: each additional shell layer adds friction, fees, and risk of exposure.
- Our cost: each additional layer of investigation costs HUMINT capital and analyst time.

## Equilibrium

In equilibrium, both parties under-invest. The adversary uses fewer shells than they could; we investigate fewer layers than we could. Both parties accept residual exposure.

## What this implies

- HERON's shell-list product ([report](../field-reports/tokyo/2026-05-06-heron-counter-acq.md)) reveals not just *who* is intermediating, but at *what depth* — i.e. how seriously they treat us.
- Avarian intermediation depth in the BoM product is modest. Either Avaria thinks we are inattentive, or the relationship is sanctioned/expected.
