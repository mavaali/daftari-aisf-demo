> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Premise

What if our entire framing of the Antiquarians is inverted? We have treated them as a laundering layer for high-value art assets — the assets are the substance; the trade is the cover. Invert.

What if the **Antiquarians are the bank** and the assets are the ledger entries?

Under this hypothesis:
- A manuscript is not a stored-value object; it is a unit of account.
- Provenance freshening is not laundering; it is account reconciliation.
- The Geneva consignment paperwork is not legitimation; it is settlement.
- The London auction is not sale; it is final transfer of title at a publicly observable mark-to-market.

## What would change?

- We would expect the volume of unsold lots (lots withdrawn) to be high — most transactions are bank-internal.
- We would expect "buyers" to recur at improbable rates — the bank has limited counterparties.
- We would expect calendar clustering — settlement windows, like a bank's month-end.

## Indicators

CARDINAL's [provenance trail](../field-reports/buenos-aires/2026-04-25-cardinal-provenance.md) is consistent with this model. The Geneva typography re-freshening pattern is consistent with this model. The Halloran-coins question ([T5](../tensions/tension-005.md)) becomes natural: a bank that handles currency notes might also handle coins.

## What to do with this

This is a moonshot — not a brief, not a finding. It is a frame we should test. The cheapest test: look for unsold-lot percentages at the Geneva house over five years.
