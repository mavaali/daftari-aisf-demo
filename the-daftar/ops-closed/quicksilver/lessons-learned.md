> **FICTIONAL · DEMO CORPUS — Bureau for Atypical Inquiry**

## Lessons (curated)

1. **HUMINT first, mapping second.** QUICKSILVER attempted to map the corridor from OSINT and archival trace alone. The result was a topology with no proof-of-flow. DOLPHIN-WAKE inverted this — HUMINT (DOLPHIN) seeded the map, then secondary sources (KESTREL, BoL anomalies) corroborated.

2. **Political-environment scoping.** QUICKSILVER under-weighted the Mumbai political environment and ran into local liaison constraints we could have predicted. Future corridor ops must include an explicit local-environment scoping step in the charter.

3. **Archival recovery as substrate.** The historical-counterparty work survived the operation's wind-down and remains usable. Long-tail value of archival product was higher than expected.

4. **Stand-down before burn.** QUICKSILVER was wound down ahead of schedule on political signal. No asset losses. Bias toward early wind-down preserves future options.
